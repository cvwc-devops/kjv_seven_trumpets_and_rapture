# Revelation 15:1, 15:6–8 Explained
## Seven Angels with the Seven Last Plagues

**Revelation 15:1** introduces the final set of judgments before Christ’s victory is fully shown.

KJV:

> “And I saw another sign in heaven, great and marvellous, seven angels having the **seven last plagues**; for in them is filled up the wrath of God.”

## Main Meaning

The **seven angels** are heavenly messengers chosen to pour out the **seven last plagues**, also called the **seven vials/bowls of God’s wrath** in Revelation 16.

They are called the **last plagues** because they complete, or finish, this phase of God’s judgment.

The phrase:

> “for in them is filled up the wrath of God”

means God’s wrath reaches its full measure. These judgments are not random disasters. In Revelation, they are presented as God’s final response to rebellion, idolatry, persecution, and refusal to repent.

## Revelation 15:6–8 Explained

KJV:

> “And the seven angels came out of the temple, having the seven plagues, clothed in pure and white linen, and having their breasts girded with golden girdles.”  
> — **Revelation 15:6**

The angels come **out of the temple**, showing these judgments come from God’s presence and authority.

Their clothing matters:

| Detail | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pure and white linen** | Holiness, purity, righteousness |
| **Golden girdles** | Royal dignity, priestly service, divine authority |
| **Coming from the temple** | Their mission comes directly from God |

Then:

> “And one of the four beasts gave unto the seven angels seven golden vials full of the wrath of God...”  
> — **Revelation 15:7**

The **seven golden vials** are bowls filled with God’s wrath. These are poured out in **Revelation 16**.

The bowls are **golden**, which shows they are connected with heaven, holiness, and God’s divine judgment. This is not corrupt anger. It is holy judgment from God.

Then:

> “And the temple was filled with smoke from the glory of God, and from his power...”  
> — **Revelation 15:8**

The smoke shows the overwhelming **glory and power of God**. This reminds us of Old Testament scenes where God’s glory filled the tabernacle or temple.

The verse ends:

> “and no man was able to enter into the temple, till the seven plagues of the seven angels were fulfilled.”

That means the judgment must now be completed. No one interrupts it. No one stops it. The seven last plagues must run their course.

---

## Simple Summary

**The seven angels with the seven last plagues represent God’s final series of judgments before the end.**

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Seven angels** | God’s appointed heavenly messengers |
| **Seven last plagues** | Final judgments of God’s wrath |
| **Temple** | God’s presence and authority |
| **White linen** | Purity and righteousness |
| **Golden girdles** | Royal/priestly authority |
| **Golden vials/bowls** | Instruments carrying God’s wrath |
| **Smoke filling the temple** | God’s glory, power, and unapproachable holiness |
| **No man entering the temple** | The judgment is fixed and must be completed |

In plain words:

**Revelation 15 shows heaven preparing for the final judgments. Revelation 16 then shows those judgments being poured out on the earth.**
