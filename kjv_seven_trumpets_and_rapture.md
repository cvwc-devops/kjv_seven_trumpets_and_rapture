# KJV Bible References: Seven Trumpets and the Rapture

## Where are the 7 trumpets in the KJV Bible?

The **seven trumpets** are in the **Book of Revelation**, mainly **Revelation chapters 8–11** in the KJV.

They appear here:

| Trumpet | KJV location |
|---|---|
| Angels receive the seven trumpets | **Revelation 8:2** |
| 1st trumpet | **Revelation 8:7** |
| 2nd trumpet | **Revelation 8:8–9** |
| 3rd trumpet | **Revelation 8:10–11** |
| 4th trumpet | **Revelation 8:12** |
| Warning before final three trumpets | **Revelation 8:13** |
| 5th trumpet / first woe | **Revelation 9:1–12** |
| 6th trumpet / second woe | **Revelation 9:13–21** |
| Interlude before the 7th trumpet | **Revelation 10:1–11:14** |
| 7th trumpet / third woe | **Revelation 11:15–19** |

The key opening verse is **Revelation 8:2 KJV**:

> “And I saw the seven angels which stood before God; and to them were given seven trumpets.”

So the short answer is: **Revelation 8:2 through Revelation 11:19**, with the actual trumpet judgments beginning at **Revelation 8:7**.

## Where is the rapture talked about in the KJV Bible?

The word **“rapture”** is **not used in the KJV Bible**, but the event people call “the rapture” is usually connected to these passages:

| Passage | Why it is used |
|---|---|
| **1 Thessalonians 4:13–18** | Believers are “caught up” to meet the Lord in the air |
| **1 Corinthians 15:51–52** | The dead are raised and the living are changed “in the twinkling of an eye” |
| **John 14:1–3** | Jesus says He will come again and receive His people unto Himself |
| **Philippians 3:20–21** | Christ changes believers’ bodies to be like His glorious body |
| **Titus 2:13** | Speaks of “that blessed hope” and Christ’s appearing |
| **Revelation 3:10** | Often cited in pre-tribulation rapture teaching: kept “from the hour of temptation” |

The clearest KJV wording is **1 Thessalonians 4:16–17**:

> “For the Lord himself shall descend from heaven with a shout… and the dead in Christ shall rise first:  
> Then we which are alive and remain shall be **caught up** together with them in the clouds, to meet the Lord in the air…”

So, in the KJV, look especially at **1 Thessalonians 4:13–18** and **1 Corinthians 15:51–52**.
